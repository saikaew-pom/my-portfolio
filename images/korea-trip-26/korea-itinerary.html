Transit Day — Incheon to Jeju

Phuket → Incheon International → Jeju Island

Today is about arrivals — and first impressions. You land at one of the world's busiest airports, catch a short domestic hop, and by evening you're standing on a volcanic island surrounded by tangerine farms and warm sea air. Don't over-plan today. Let Jeju ease you in gently.
LunchAirport food court (try gimbap or ramyeon)
DinnerDongmun Market street food
Incheon International Airport
Morning — ArrivalTransit
One of Asia's top airports — a city in itself

Incheon consistently ranks among the best airports on earth, and it shows. There are art galleries, a Korean cultural experience centre, a golf course, and more food options than most city centres. If you have a 3–4 hour layover, it won't feel like a wait at all.

Practical tips
Domestic flights to Jeju depart from Terminal 1 — allow 90 min transfer time
T'money card available at convenience stores — top it up for Jeju taxis
Try Lotteria or Paris Baguette for a quick cheap bite before boarding
Jeju City — Arrive & Settle
Evening — Check InArrival
Your first breath of island air

Jeju's airport is tiny and friendly — you'll be out and in a taxi within 20 minutes of landing. The island greets you with volcanic rock walls, tangerine trees along every road, and air that smells cleaner than anything back home.

Practical tips
Kakao T app is the easiest way to hail taxis on Jeju — download before you land
Central Jeju City hotels are the best base for Days 1–3
Dongmun Traditional Market
7:00 PMNight Market
Jeju's oldest market, alive after dark — since 1945

Dongmun has been the beating heart of Jeju City since 1945. By day it's a fish and produce market for locals. By night, Gate 8 transforms into a glowing corridor of street food stalls — vendors calling out, seafood sizzling, and the smell of hotteok drifting through the crowd. Your first Korean street food moment: don't rush it.

What to eat
Jeju black pork skewers (흑돼지) — Jeju's signature ingredient
Fresh tangerine juice — Jeju grows the best in Korea
Hotteok — sweet stuffed pancakes, crispy outside, molten inside
Cash only at most stalls — bring ₩30,000–50,000 for a relaxed evening

Jeju East — Sunrise Peak & Lava Cave

An early start rewards you with Jeju's most dramatic scenery

This is the day that will define your Jeju trip. You'll wake in the dark, hike to the rim of a 180,000-year-old volcanic crater to watch the sun rise over the sea, then descend into a lava tube that runs beneath the island like a frozen river of rock. Two worlds — sky and underground — in one single day.
BreakfastConvenience store before departure (onigiri + coffee)
LunchRestaurant near Manjanggul — haemul jjigae (seafood stew)
DinnerDongmun food street — fire show tteokbokki & grilled lobster
Pre-dawn Departure to Seongsan
5:00 AM — Taxi ~40 minEarly Start
Beat the crowds to the most photographed spot in Jeju

The drive east from Jeju City in pre-dawn darkness is already atmospheric. Coastal road, occasional lighthouse, the sea invisible but present. Book your taxi the night before via Kakao T — state you need a 5 AM pickup.

Tips
Grab a convenience store onigiri and coffee before you leave
Wear layers — pre-dawn at the coast can be cool even in June
Seongsan Ilchulbong — Sunrise Peak
5:30 AM — 2 hoursUNESCO Hike
Sunrise over a 100,000-year-old volcanic crater — ₩5,000 entry

Seongsan Ilchulbong erupted from the sea around 100,000 years ago and now stands 182 metres tall, its vast crater still intact. The climb is only 20–25 minutes but steep — stone steps that feel like a staircase to the clouds. At the top, the crater stretches out below you, impossibly green, and beyond it the East China Sea lights up as the sun breaks the horizon. This is why you woke at 5 AM.

Tips
Gates open 5:00 AM — June sunrise is around 5:15–5:30 AM, arrive early
Wear sport shoes — the steps are uneven and can be slippery with morning dew
The view looking back toward the coast is just as stunning as the crater view
Entry: ₩5,000 per person
Udo Island — Optional Morning Detour
8:30 AM — 2 hoursIsland
Tiny island off Seongsan: white coral beaches & peanut ice cream

Just 15 minutes by ferry from Seongsan port, Udo is a quiet island famous for its snow-white Hongjo Beach and haenyeo (diving women) culture. A 2-hour loop by scooter or electric bike is magical if you have the energy after the sunrise hike.

Tips
Ferry runs from ~8 AM, return last ferry at 6 PM — ₩6,500 round trip
Skip if you'd rather rest after the early wake-up — Manjanggul is the main event today
Manjanggul Lava Tube
11:00 AM — 1.5 hoursUNESCO Cave
One of the world's longest lava tubes — cool 18°C underground

When Hallasan volcano erupted hundreds of thousands of years ago, rivers of lava carved tunnels through the earth as the outer layers cooled and hardened. Today you walk 1 km through that tunnel — cathedral-high ceilings, frozen lava shelves, and at the far end the world's largest lava column standing 7.6 metres tall. The temperature drops 10°C the moment you enter. After the morning heat of Sunrise Peak, it feels like stepping into another dimension.

Tips
Entry ₩4,000 per person
Bring a light jacket — 18°C can feel cold after sweating on the morning hike
Wear closed shoes — the floor is raw volcanic rock, uneven and sometimes wet
Allow 1–1.5 hours for the full 1 km walk and return
Dongmun Market — Fire Show Night
6:30 PM — EveningNight Market
Second visit — grilled lobster & the famous tteokbokki fire show

You saw Dongmun on your first night. Tonight you know what you're doing. Head straight for the famous fire show stall where the vendor performs a theatrical cooking display with crab tteokbokki and grilled lobster. The crowd gathers, the flames go up, and the food is genuinely excellent.

What to order
Whole grilled lobster (랍스터) — theatrical and delicious
Crab tteokbokki — the fire show is the main performance here
The "Handsome Youngmen" stall (청년미남들) has the best energy in the market
Best after 7 PM when all stalls are running at full energy

Jeju Nature — Healing Forest & Aewol Coast

Jeju's quieter, more romantic side

Yesterday was dramatic. Today is about breathing. You'll walk through a forest of 800-year-old trees that locals believe have healing properties, then follow the coastline west to Aewol — a stretch of volcanic rock, crashing waves, and stylish cafés that has become Jeju's most beloved couple destination.
BreakfastHotel or café in Jeju City
LunchMaeil Olle Market near Bijarim Forest
DinnerOllye Juk — Jeju traditional bistro, Seogwipo
Bijarim Healing Forest
9:00 AM — 2 hoursAncient Forest
800-year-old nutmeg trees and a forest that locals call sacred

Bijarim is the only nutmeg (bija) forest in Korea, and it feels genuinely different from ordinary forests. The trees — some over 800 years old and 25 metres tall — grow with an eerie symmetry, their roots knuckled into dark volcanic soil. Visitors walk in near-silence here. There's a 30-minute loop and a 3-hour trail; the shorter one captures the magic without exhausting you before the coastal walk.

Tips
Entry: ₩1,500, opens 7 AM
The 30-minute course is ideal — shorter trail hits the most beautiful section
Zero phone signal inside — leave it in your pocket and actually look at the trees
Maeil Olle Market — Lunch
11:30 AMLocal Market
Small authentic market loved by locals, not tourists

This is the market that Jeju residents actually shop at — modest, unhurried, honest food. Grab a proper Korean lunch here after the forest walk. The shrimp vegetable pancake at stalls near the entrance is something special.

What to try
Shrimp pancake — crispy, fresh, dip in the tangy sauce provided
Grilled fish set — Jeju seafood at its most honest
Cash-based — bring ₩15,000–20,000 for a full lunch
Aewol Handam Coastal Walk
3:00 PM — 2–3 hoursCoastal Trail
Volcanic coastline, windmills, and a café street that stops time

The Aewol coastal trail is Jeju at its most quietly beautiful. The path runs along raw black volcanic rock at the edge of the sea, past old windmills that have turned here for generations. As you reach the Aewol café district, the trail opens into a curved street of independent coffee shops — each one perched above the water with floor-to-ceiling windows. Sit down. Watch the waves. Stay longer than you planned.

Tips
Open 24 hours, but 3–6 PM is the golden hour light window for photos
Allow 2–3 hours minimum for a relaxed walk and café stop
Wear comfortable shoes — the volcanic rock path has uneven sections
Café Monsant and Caffe Bora are both on this strip — worth the queue
Ollye Juk — Jeju Traditional Bistro
7:00 PM — DinnerDinner
Cosy traditional Korean dinner with locally-sourced Jeju ingredients

This small bistro near Seogwipo has quietly become a favourite among food-aware travellers. The shrimp vegetable pancake is crispy and delicate; the fried pork slices arrive golden; the bean paste tofu soup warms you from the inside. No frills, no pretension — just honest, delicious food.

Tips
Reservations recommended — small space, fills up by 7:30 PM
Try the pork set for two — includes pancake, grilled fish, and soup
The radish banchan with black pepper is unusual and genuinely addictive

Fly Jeju → Seoul + Gwangjang Market

Morning farewell to the island, evening arrival in the capital

Your last morning in Jeju is bittersweet — you'll be back someday. The domestic flight to Seoul is barely an hour, but the transition is total: from volcanic island silence to the buzzing, neon-lit density of Korea's 10-million-person capital. Tonight you dive straight into one of Seoul's most legendary food experiences.
BreakfastHotel or Jeju airport café
LunchGimpo Airport on arrival
DinnerGwangjang Market — full food crawl
Check Out & Fly to Seoul
8:00 AM — ~55 min flightDeparture
Short 1-hour domestic hop from Jeju to Seoul Gimpo Airport

Jeju Airport is wonderfully small and calm. Domestic security takes 10 minutes. Buy one last bag of Jeju tangerine chocolate at the duty-free kiosks before you board — far cheaper here than anywhere in Seoul.

Tips
Gimpo (GMP) is closer to central Seoul than Incheon — you'll be at your hotel by noon
Buy Jeju-specific souvenirs here: tangerine chocolate, black sesame crackers
Check In — Seoul Hotel
12:00 PM — AfternoonCheck-in
Base yourself in Myeongdong, Insadong, or Hongdae for Days 4–6

For a couple on a mid-range budget, the sweet spot is Myeongdong or Insadong — central, walkable, and close to everything on the itinerary. Get rest, freshen up, and save your energy for the evening market.

Tips
Recommended: Myeongdong (shopping access), Insadong (quieter, cultural)
Kakao T works perfectly in Seoul — your default taxi app for the rest of the trip
Get a SIM or pocket WiFi at Gimpo if you haven't already
Gwangjang Market — Dinner
5:00 PM — EveningHistoric Market
Seoul's oldest covered market, open since 1905

Gwangjang Market has been feeding Seoul since 1905, and the food section in the evening is one of the city's greatest sensory experiences. The central food hall is lined with grandmother vendors who have been making the same dishes for decades. The most famous: bindaetteok — thick, savoury mung bean pancakes fried crispy on a cast-iron griddle, best eaten with makgeolli (rice wine). At the far entrance, a stall always has a 30-person queue for its twisted glutinous rice donut. The queue moves fast. Get in it.

Must-eat list
Bindaetteok (mung bean pancake) + makgeolli pairing — the definitive Gwangjang combo
Twisted rice donut stall at the main entrance — tiny, cheap, perfect
Mayak gimbap — mini kimbap rolls with mustard dipping sauce, addictive
Yukhoe — Korean beef tartare if you're adventurous
Cash only at most stalls — bring ₩40,000–60,000 for a full evening

Seoul History — Palaces, Hanok Village & Night Market

The full cultural Seoul experience in one perfect day

Today is the day Seoul reveals its depth. You'll walk through a 15th-century royal palace still standing in the heart of a modern megacity, wander hillside alleyways where traditional wooden homes have survived wars and demolitions, browse an artisan street that has refused to become a mall, and end the night eating skewers in a market that lights up like a carnival.
BreakfastHotel or café near accommodation
LunchNear Bukchon — bibimbap or sundubu jjigae
DinnerMyeongdong Night Market — full street food run
Gyeongbokgung Palace
9:00 AM — 2.5 hoursRoyal Palace
The 'Palace of Shining Happiness' — built 1395, still breathtaking

Gyeongbokgung was the heart of the Joseon Dynasty for over 500 years. The Japanese burned it in 1592. The Koreans rebuilt it. The Japanese demolished most of it during colonial occupation. The Koreans rebuilt it again — and are still rebuilding it today. What stands now is a testament to cultural stubbornness: 13 restored structures on a 400,000 m² compound. Rent a Hanbok at the south gate — entry becomes free and your photos will be extraordinary.

Tips
Arrives early at 9 AM — tour groups flood in after 10
Hanbok rental: ₩15,000–20,000 for 2–3 hours — wearers enter FREE
Royal Guard Changing Ceremony: 10 AM and 2 PM at Gwanghwamun Gate
Closed Tuesdays — we scheduled this for Wednesday, no issue!
Bukchon Hanok Village
11:30 AM — 1.5 hoursLiving Heritage
600-year-old wooden homes still lived in today

Bukchon sits on the hillside between two palaces and has been a residential neighbourhood for Korean aristocracy since the Joseon era. Today, around 900 hanok survive here — many converted into guesthouses or tea rooms, but many still private family homes. The alleyway views from Bukchon-ro 11-gil are among the most photographed in all of Korea. Walk quietly — people actually live here.

Tips
10-minute walk from Gyeongbokgung's northeast gate — perfect morning combo
The iconic viewpoint: Bukchon-ro 11-gil (signed 'Bukchon Viewpoint' on maps)
Arrive before 11 AM to avoid afternoon crowds
Visiting hours: 10 AM – 5 PM only — residential area, please be quiet
Insadong Culture Street
1:30 PM — 2 hoursArts District
The street that refused to become a shopping mall

While most of Seoul's traditional commercial streets were bulldozed for glass towers, Insadong held on. Today it's a long pedestrian street lined with art galleries, traditional tea houses hidden in courtyards, celadon pottery shops, and calligraphy stalls. Hidden inside Ssamziegil courtyard is a winding spiral walkway of independent studios. The kind of place where you accidentally spend three hours browsing ceramics and eating street waffles.

Tips
Ssamziegil courtyard: look for the entrance at 44 Insadong-gil
Best souvenir: handmade hanji notebooks or small celadon pieces
Try egg bread (계란빵) from street carts — warm, eggy, comforting
Open from 10 AM, most atmospheric after 11 AM on weekdays
Myeongdong Night Market
6:30 PM — Late NightNight Market
Seoul's most electric street food strip — open until 1 AM

Myeongdong by day is a shopping district. By night it's a different animal entirely. From 6 PM the vendors set up — hundreds of them, all the way down the main pedestrian street and into side lanes. The food is louder here than anywhere else in Seoul: vendors call out, giant portions steam in the cold air, tourists and locals queue side by side for the same skewers.

Must-eat list
Tanghulu — candy-coated fruit skewers, essential Myeongdong experience
Cheese corn dog — look for the queue, it's there for a reason
Haemul pajeon (seafood scallion pancake) from side-street sit-down stalls
Tteokbokki — spicy rice cakes, the quintessential Korean street snack
Cash preferred — some carts accept card, most don't

Final Morning + Fly Home to Phuket

Last bites, last photos, then back home

Every great trip has a goodbye ritual. Yours is standing at Gwanghwamun Gate in the soft morning light, the palace behind you and Seoul's skyline ahead, before the city wakes up. Then duty-free snacks, a window seat, and the long reflection on six days that felt like much more.
BreakfastHotel breakfast or café near accommodation
SnackAirport duty-free — Korean snacks for the flight home
Gwanghwamun Gate — Morning Walk
9:00 AM — 45 minFarewell Stop
The great gate of Joseon, standing since 1395

Gwanghwamun is the main gate of Gyeongbokgung Palace and has survived Japanese colonial erasure, the Korean War, and six centuries of history. In the morning light, before tourist crowds arrive, it's quietly magnificent — stone and painted wood framed by the Bugaksan mountain and Sejongno boulevard stretching south. Take your best couple photo of the trip. Say goodbye to Seoul properly.

Tips
Open 24 hours — no entry fee for the gate area
The Sejong Story underground exhibition is free and surprisingly worthwhile
Changing of guards at the gate: 10 AM if your flight allows time
Head to Incheon Airport
11:00 AM — Allow 3+ hoursDeparture
AREX express: Seoul Station → Incheon T1 in 43 minutes

The AREX express train from Seoul Station runs directly to Incheon Terminal 1 in 43 minutes — clean, cheap (₩9,500), and perfectly timed. Or take a taxi if you're carrying heavy luggage and prefer door-to-door. Allow a full 3 hours before your international departure.

Best duty-free buys
Korean red ginseng — the gold standard souvenir
Honey butter almonds — dangerously addictive, hard to find outside Korea
Choco Pies (big box) — the iconic Korean snack
Jeju tangerine snacks if you didn't buy enough at the island
One last cup of Korean convenience store coffee before you board
